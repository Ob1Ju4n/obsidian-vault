### Situation
- The adventurers left [[Phandalin]] towards the ambush site in the Triboar trail.
- Their goal is to find out the whereabouts of [[Gundren Rockseeker]] and his escort but they first need to reach the ambush site.

### Development

> [[Balian]] Follow a Path (5, 10 vs 3 + 5 Supply) = Weak Hit

As they get close to the site, [[Balian]] decides to go slower. He doesn't want to be surprised again. It takes them almost 7 hours to get back to the ambush site. They could've spent almost a full day on the trail if not for [[Alderan]] who was rather insistent on the fact that the more time they spent, the harder it would be to locate the dwarf.

[[Balian]] quickly notices a path behind some thickets and demands that the horses stay. While reluctant, [[Alderan]] knows that it will take him longer trying to convince the ranger than moving along. As they start walking, the ranger asks the bard to avoid making any music or loud sounds. An almost impossible endeavor for the human as he needs to talk as much as a flower  needs water in the summer.

That's when the elf notices an odd change of color on the ground. [[Alderan]] sees the improvised tapestry and takes his wooden staff trying to check what's the deal, only to reveal a pit trap that's almost 10ft deep. The elf looks at him again but the human just nods and keeps silent.

The sunlight starts to dimm and the adventurers see in the distance what appears to be a cave on the base of a hill. [[Alderan]] moves ahead unaware of the patrol that's making its last round on the trail. It's the elf's sharp eyes that notice within the patrol a wolf guiding the Goblins. A wild creature with a keen sense of smell. 

Knowing that the situation can turn for the worst, he grabs the bard, pulls him back and moves away to extend the distance from the animal and its masters.

> [[Alderan]] Face Danger (7, 4 vs ~~1~~ 7 Momentum burn + 3 Edge) = Strong hit

The elf mentions with a serious voice that they need to move quickly. He knows very well that the closer they're, the easier it will be for the wolf to track them. The bard understands the threat but at the end of the day its just an animal, he thinks. And so, he pulls his lute and stands in the middle of the bushes while tuning the strings. The elf urges him to move but the human stays there with a smile. Two minor chords and a couple of rhymes after, around 30ft from them, the screams of a boy asking for help echoes against the trees. This catches the attention of the patrol who quickly move towards the source of the sound, pushing the chain on the wolves neck against its best interests. With just enough time, the duo is able to escape, going deeper into the forest where the embrace of the night covers with shadows the surroundings.
