After a couple minutes, the bartender —a female, red haired dwarf— approaches them with a message. She kindly asks them to go to the wooden desk, where the Inkeeper is waving. The pair stand up and walk to the entrance where a middle-aged human male greets them and introduces himself as [[Toblen Stonehill]], owner of the inn. After a brief greeting, he lets them know that the real reason he is calling them was so he could politely ask them to leave to another room.

The bard is offended as this feels unfair to them. He raises one finger and with tension in his voice, demands an explanation. As he raises his voice, a child comes from the room behind the Innkeeper asking his father if everything is alright. [[Toblen Stonehill|Toblen]] smiles and dismisses him but then asks [[Alderan]] to calm down while explaining that no one in the dinning room wants to hear about [[The Redbrands Gang]]. They kind of own the town and he wants to stay out of trouble and keep everyone safe, his family above everything, specially after witnessing how they murdered Thel, a fellow neighbor.

Suddenly, the anguish runs through [[Alderan|Alderan's]] face as he remembers what [[Elmina Barthen]] was up to. [[Balian]], who is more upset than anxious, takes a step forward and tells the Innkeeper that he will do as requested but needs details about the ruffians, as much as he or anyone can give.

> [[Balian]] Compel (5, 6 vs 6 + 2 Heart) = Strong hit

> [[Balian]] Gather Information (1, 5 vs 4 + 1 Compel + 3 Wits) = Strong hit

While [[Toblen Stonehill|Toblen]] hesitates, the innocent voice of the kid breaks the silence, mentioning that his friend "Pip" found a secret tunnel in the woods and was almost caught by [[The Redbrands Gang|The Redbrands]].

Startled and ashamed, [[Toblen Stonehill|Toblen]] scolds his kid and sends him back to the kitchen. After recovering his composure, he walks the adventurers outside the inn. There he cleans the sweat from his neck and reveals that the ruffians usually gather at "The Sleeping Giant" and that Thel's wife and kids have gone missing after the woodcarver demise. He doesn't know any other details but before returning to work, he remembers that there's one specific business that they've not messed with. It's the Phandalin Miner's Exchange, lead by [[Halia Thornton]]. Having said that, the innkeeper excuses himself back into the inn.

While the information is most welcome, hunger still remains. They end up sharing some rations from their backpacks and set forth towards the center of the town while they discuss their next steps. [[Alderan]] mentions that he would like to talk to [[Elmina Barthen|Elmina]] as she knows the town and possibly has an idea to why the bandits avoid [[Halia Thornton|Halia's]] business. Understanding that the bard is also  worried for her well-being, [[Balian]]  follows while thinking on the other pieces of information.
